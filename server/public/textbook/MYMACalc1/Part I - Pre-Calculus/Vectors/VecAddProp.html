5. Vectors

b. Vector Addition

3. Properties

We here list the algebraic properties of vector addition and its relation to magnitude.

In the following, \(\vec u\), \(\vec v\), and \(\vec w\) are vectors. Also, \(\vec 0=\langle0,0\rangle\) is the vector whose components are both zero and \(-\vec v=\langle-v_1,-v_2\rangle\) denotes the vector whose components are the negatives of those for \(\vec v\).

First, there are four properties involving just vector addition.

Let \(\vec u\), \(\vec v\), and \(\vec w\) be arbitrary vectors. Then,

  1. Addition is Commutative:   \(\vec u+\vec v=\vec v+\vec u\)  

\[\begin{aligned} \vec u+\vec v &=\left\langle u_1,u_2\right\rangle+\left\langle v_1,v_2\right\rangle =\left\langle u_1+v_1,u_2+v_2\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle v_1+u_1,v_2+u_2\right\rangle =\left\langle v_1,v_2\right\rangle+\left\langle u_1,u_2\right\rangle \\ &=\vec v+\vec u \end{aligned}\] A geometrical proof was also given on the previous page.

  1. Addition is Associative:   \((\vec u+\vec v)+\vec w=\vec u+(\vec v+\vec w)\)  

\[\begin{aligned} (\vec u+\vec v)+\vec w &=\left(\left\langle u_1,u_2\right\rangle+\left\langle v_1,v_2\right\rangle\right) +\left\langle w_1,w_2\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle u_1+v_1,u_2+v_2\right\rangle+\left\langle w_1,w_2\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle u_1+v_1+w_1,u_2+v_2+w_2\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle u_1,u_2\right\rangle+\left\langle v_1+w_1,v_2+w_2\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle u_1,u_2\right\rangle +\left(\left\langle v_1,v_2\right\rangle+\left\langle w_1,w_2\right\rangle\right) \\ &=\vec u+(\vec v+\vec w) \end{aligned}\]

  1. \(\vec 0\) is the Zero or Additive Identity:   \(\vec v+\vec 0=\vec v\)  

\[\begin{aligned} \vec v+\vec 0 &=\left\langle v_1,v_2\right\rangle+\left\langle0,0\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle v_1+0,v_2+0\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle v_1,v_2\right\rangle \\ &=\vec v \end{aligned}\]

  1. \(-\vec v\) is the Negative or Additive Inverse of \(\vec v\):   \(\vec v+(-\vec v)=\vec 0\)  

\[\begin{aligned} \vec v+(-\vec v) &=\left\langle v_1,v_2\right\rangle+\left\langle-v_1,-v_2\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle v_1-v_1,v_2-v_2\right\rangle \\ &=\left\langle0,0\right\rangle=\vec 0 \end{aligned}\]

Here are three additional properties that you might want to prove. They can be proved using only properties 1-4 in the above Theorem without writing out any components. They also appear in the exercises.

  1. The Zero Vector, \(\vec 0\), is Unique:
       If \(\vec z\) is a vector which satisfies \(\vec v+\vec z=\vec v\), then \(\vec z=\vec 0\).
  2. The Negative, \(-\vec v\), is Unique:
       Given a vector \(\vec v\), if there is a vector \(\vec w\) satisfying \(\vec v+\vec w=\vec 0\), then \(\vec w=-\vec v\).
  3. Additive Cancellation:
       If \(\vec u+\vec v=\vec w+\vec v\), then \(\vec u=\vec w\).
