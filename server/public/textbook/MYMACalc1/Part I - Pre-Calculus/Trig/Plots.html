Trigonometry

Plots of Trig Functions

We would like to find the plots of the six trig functions.

Sine and Cosine Plots

The plot shows a unit circle, (i.e. \(r=1\)) with a radial line at an angle \(\theta\) counterclockwise from the positive \(x\)-axis. The radial line intersects the circle at a point \(P=(x,y)\). So the sine and cosine are: \[ \sin\theta=\dfrac{y}{r}=y \qquad \cos\theta=\dfrac{x}{r}=x \]

So to plot \(\sin\theta\) and \(\cos\theta\), we need to plot \(y\) and \(x\) as \(\theta\) changes. This is done in the animation. As \(\theta\) increases, \(y\) goes up and down which is graphed at the right and \(x\) goes left and right which is graphed below.
(Tilt your head to the right for cosine. )

Tangent and Cotangent Plots

The first plot shows a unit circle, (i.e. \(r=1\)) with a radial line at an angle \(\theta\) counterclockwise from the positive \(x\)-axis and a vertical tangent line at the right side. The radial line intersects the tangent line at a point \(Q=(x,y)\). Since the circle has radius \(1\), we know \(x=1\). So the tangent is: \[ \tan\theta=\dfrac{y}{x}=y \] The second plot shows the same unit circle but with a horizontal tangent line at the top. The radial line intersects the tangent line at a point \(R=(x,y)\). Since the circle has radius \(1\), we know \(y=1\). So the cotangent is: \[ \cot\theta=\dfrac{x}{y}=x \]

So to plot \(\tan\theta\) and \(\cot\theta\), we need to plot the \(y\) coordinate of \(Q\) on the vertical tangent and the \(x\) coordinate of \(R\) on the horizontal tangent. This is done in the animation. As \(\theta\) increases, \(y\) goes up which is graphed at the right and \(x\) goes left which is graphed below.
(Tilt your head to the right for cotangent. )

Secant and Cosecant Plots

The plot shows a unit circle, (i.e. \(r=1\)) with a radial line at an angle \(\theta\) counterclockwise from the positive \(x\)-axis together with the tangent line at the point \(P\) at the end of the radial line. The tangent line intersects the \(x\)-axis at a point \(Q=(x,0)\) and intersects the \(y\)-axis at a point \(R=(0,y)\). Looking at the triangle \(\triangle OPQ\), the angle at \(O\) is \(\theta\), the adjacent side is the radius \(r=1\) and the hypotenuse is \(x\). So the secant is: \[ \sec\theta=\dfrac{x}{r}=x \] Looking at the triangle \(\triangle OPR\), the angle at \(R\) is \(\theta\), the opposite side is the radius \(r=1\) and the hypotenuse is \(y\). So the cosecant is: \[ \csc\theta=\dfrac{y}{r}=y \]

So to plot \(\sec\theta\) and \(\csc\theta\), we need to plot the \(x\) coordinate of \(Q\) and the \(y\) coordinate of \(R\) as \(\theta\) changes. This is done in the animation. As \(\theta\) increases, \(x\) goes left and then right which is graphed below and \(y\) goes down and then up which is graphed at the right.
(Tilt your head to the right for secant. )
