    1. Find the average velocity, \(v\) between \(t=5\) and \(t=6\)

      Remember that the speed(e.g., velocity) is the rate of change between 2 points.

      The average velocity between \(t=5\) and \(t=6\) is \(125\) ft/sec.

      We find the rate of change (or velocity, \(v\)) between the points \(t=5\) and \(t=6\) to obtain the average velocity: \[\begin{aligned} v&=\dfrac{f(5)-f(6)}{5-6} \\ &=-([400(5)-25(5)^2]-[400(6)-25(6)^2]) \\ &=[400(6)-25(6)^2]-[400(5)-25(5)^2] \\ &=[400(6-5) -25(6+5)(6-5) \\ &=400-275=125 \end{aligned}\] Therefore the average velocity between \(t=5\) and \(t=6\) is \(125\) ft/sec.

  3. \(f(x)=x^3\) at \(a=2\).

    There are 2 formulas for computing the derivative of the function \(f(x)\) at the point \(x=a\): \[ f'(a)=\lim_{h\to0} \dfrac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h} \] \[ f'(a)=\lim_{x\to a} \dfrac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a} \] Use whichever formula is convenient.

    Also remember: \[ (a+h)^3=a^3+3a^2h+3ah^2+h^3 \]

    \(f'(2)=12\)

    Solve for the limit by using the first method. In this case, \(f(x)=x^3\) and \(a=2\). \[\begin{aligned} f'(2)&=\lim_{h\to0} \dfrac{f(2+h)-f(2)}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\to0} \dfrac{(2+h)^3 - 8}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\to0} \dfrac{12h + 6h^2 + h^3}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\to0} (12+6h + h^2) = 12 \end{aligned}\]

We do the limit by multiplying by the conjugate. \[\begin{aligned} B'(t)&=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{B(t+h)-B(t)}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(t+h)^{3/2}-t^{3/2}}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(t+h)^{3/2}-t^{3/2}}{h} \cdot\dfrac{(t+h)^{3/2}+t^{3/2}}{(t+h)^{3/2}+t^{3/2}} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(t+h)^3-t^3}{h[(t+h)^{3/2}+t^{3/2}]} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{3t^2h+3th^2+h^3}{h[(t+h)^{3/2}+t^{3/2}]} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{3t^2+3th+h^2}{(t+h)^{3/2}+t^{3/2}} \\ &=\dfrac{3t^2}{t^{3/2}+t^{3/2}} =\dfrac{3t^2}{2t^{3/2}} =\dfrac{3}{2}t^{1/2} =\dfrac{3}{2}\sqrt{t} \end{aligned}\] So, the rate at \(t=9\) is: \[\begin{aligned} B'(9)=\dfrac{3}{2}\sqrt{9}=\dfrac{9}{2} \end{aligned}\]
