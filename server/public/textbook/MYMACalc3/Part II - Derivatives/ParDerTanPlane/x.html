5. Partial Derivatives and Tangent Planes

Exercises

    a. Computation of Partial Derivatives

    1

  1. Find \(f_x\) for the function: \(f(x,y)=x^2y+xy^2\).

    \(f_x=2xy+y^2\)

    We differentiate \(f(x,y)\) with respect to \(x\) assuming \(y\) is a constant: \[ f_x=\dfrac{\partial}{\partial x}(x^2y+xy^2)=2xy+y^2 \]

    dm,jb 

  2. Find \(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}\) and \(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}\) for the function: \(f(x,y)=y\sin x+x\sin y+\sin(xy)+2\).

    \(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}=y\cos x+\sin y+y\cos(xy)\)
    \(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}=\sin x+x\cos y+x\cos(xy)\)

    To find \(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}\), we differentiate \(f(x,y)\) with respect to \(x\), while keeping \(y\) constant: \[ \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}=y\cos x+\sin y+y\cos(xy) \] Similarly, we find \(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}\) by differentiating \(f(x,y)\) with respect to \(y\), while keeping \(x\) constant: \[ \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}=\sin x+x\cos y+x\cos(xy) \]

    dm,jb 

  3. Consider the function \(f=x(\ln y)\sin(xy)+\cos(xy)\).

    1. Find \(f_x\).

      \(f_x=(\ln y)\sin(xy)+xy(\ln y)\cos(xy)-y\sin(xy)\)

      We differentiate \(f\) with respect to \(x\) holding \(y\) constant: \[ f_x=(\ln y)\sin(xy)+xy(\ln y)\cos(xy)-y\sin(xy) \]

      dm 

    2. Find \(f_y\).

      \(f_y=\dfrac{x\sin(xy)}{y}+x^2(\ln y)\cos(xy)-x\sin(xy)\)

      We differentiate \(f\) with respect to \(y\) holding \(x\) constant: \[ f_y=\dfrac{x\sin(xy)}{y}+x^2(\ln y)\cos(xy)-x\sin(xy) \]

      dm 

    4. b. Algebraic Definition of Partial Derivatives

    1

  4. Find the \(x\)-partial derivative of \(f(x,y)=x^2y^3\) using the limit definition of partial derivatives.

    \(f_x(x,y)=2xy^3\)

    According to the algebraic definition of partial derivatives: \[\begin{aligned} f_x(x,y) &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{f(x+h,y)-f(x,y)}{h} =\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(x+h)^2y^3-x^2y^3}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{(x^2+2hx+h^2)y^3-x^2y^3}{h} =\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{2hxy^3+h^2y^3}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \left(2xy^3+hy^3\right)=2xy^3 \end{aligned}\]

    dm,jb 

  5. Find the value of \(\displaystyle \lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin(xy^2+2xyh+xh^2)-\sin(xy^2)}{h}\).

    \(\displaystyle \lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin(xy^2+2xyh+xh^2)-\sin(xy^2)}{h} =2xy\cos(xy^2)\)

    \[\begin{aligned} \lim_{h\rightarrow 0} &\dfrac{\sin(xy^2+2xyh+xh^2)-\sin(xy^2)}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin(x(y^2+2yh+h^2))-\sin(xy^2)}{h} \\ &=\lim_{h\rightarrow 0} \dfrac{\sin(x(y+h)^2)-\sin(xy^2)}{h} \end{aligned}\] According to the algebraic definition of partial derivatives, this is equal to the \(y\)-partial derivative of \(\sin(xy^2)\): \[ =\dfrac{\partial}{\partial y}\sin(xy^2) =2xy\cos(xy^2) \]

    dm,jb 

    6. c. Geometric Interpretation of Partial Derivatives

    1
  6. For the graph of the function \(f=x^2y+\dfrac{x}{y}\), compute

    1. the slope of the \(x\)-trace at the point \((2,1)\).

      \(f_x(2,1)=5\)

      First, we find the partial derivative with respect to \(x\): \[ f_x=2xy+\dfrac{1}{y} \]

      Then we plug in the point \((2,1)\): \[f_x(2,1)=4+\dfrac{1}{1}=5\]

      dm,jb 

    2. the tangent line to the \(x\)-trace at the point \((2,1)\).

      \(z=5x-4\)

      We plug the point \((a,b)=(2,1)\) into the function: \[ f(2,1)=2^2 1+\dfrac{2}{1}=6 \] Using this and the \(x\)-slope, \(f_x(2,1)=5\), we find the tangent line: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f(a,b)+f_x(a,b)(x-a) \\ z&=f(2,1)+f_x(2,1)(x-2) \\ &=6+5(x-2) \\ &=5x-4 \end{aligned}\]

      dm,jb 

    3. the slope of the \(y\)-trace at the point \((2,1)\).

      \(f_y(2,1)=2\)

      First, we find the partial derivative with respect to \(y\): \[ f_y=x^2-\,\dfrac{x}{y^2} \] Then we plug in the point \((2,1)\): \[ f_y(2,1)=4-\,\dfrac{2}{1}=2 \]

      dm,jb 

    4. the tangent line to the \(y\)-trace at the point \((2,1)\).

      \(z=2y+4\)

      We plug the point \((a,b)=(2,1)\) into the function: \[ f(2,1)=2^2 1+\dfrac{2}{1}=6 \] Using this and the \(y\)-slope, \(f_y(2,1)=2\), we find the tangent line: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f(a,b)+f_y(a,b)(y-b) \\ z&=f(2,1)+f_y(2,1)(y-1) \\ &=6+2(y-1) \\ &=2y+4 \end{aligned}\]

      dm,jb 

    7. d. Tangent Plane to the Graph of a Function

    1
    2

  7. For the graph of the function \(f=x^2y+\dfrac{x}{y}\), discussed in a previous exercise, find the equation of the plane tangent at \((2,1)\). Then, find the \(z\)-intercept of the plane.

    Evaluate the function and its partial derivatives at \((x,y)=(2,1)\). Then use the formula: \[ z=f(a,b)+f_x(a,b)(x-a)+f_y(a,b)(y-b) \]

    \(z=5x+2y-6\)
    The \(z\)-intercept is \(-6\).

    In the previous exercise, we found \[\begin{aligned} f(2,1)&=6 \\ f_x(2,1)&=5 \\ f_y(2,1)&=2 \\ \end{aligned}\] We plug these into the formula: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f(a,b)+f_x(a,b)(x-a)+f_y(a,b)(y-b) \\ z&=f(2,1)+f_x(2,1)(x-2)+f_y(2,1)(y-1) \\ &=6+5(x-2)+2(y-1) \\ &=5x+2y-6 \end{aligned}\] The \(z\)-intercept is \(-6\).

    dm,jb 

    We have found the tangent plane at \((x,y)=(2,1)\) is: \(z=5x+2y-6\).
    Plugging in \(y=1\), the tangent line to the \(x\)-trace is: \(z=5x-4\).
    Plugging in \(x=2\), the tangent line to the \(y\)-trace is: \(z=2y+4\).
    These agree with the previous exercise.

  8. Find the equation of the plane tangent to \(z=x^2+y^3\) at the point \((x,y)=(2,1)\). Then find its \(z\)-intercept.

    Find the values of the function and its partial derivatives at \((2,1)\).
    Then plug into the formula for the tangent plane: \[ z=f(a,b)+f_x(a,b)(x-a)+f_y(a,b)(y-b) \] Read off the \(z\)-intercept \(c\) from the equation \(z=mx+ny+c\).

    The tangent plane is \(z=4x+3y-6\).
    The \(z\)-intercept is \(-6\).

    We identify \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^3\), \(a=2\) and \(b=1\). For the tangent plane equation, we need \(f(2,1)\), \(f_x(2,1)\), and \(f_y(2,1)\). It is good to make a table: \[\begin{aligned} &f=x^2+y^3 \qquad &f(2,1)=5 \\ &f_x=2x \qquad &f_x(2,1)=4 \\ &f_y=3y^2 \qquad &f_y(2,1)=3 \end{aligned}\] So the equation of the tangent plane is: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f(2,1)+f_x(2,1)(x-2)+f_y(2,1)(y-1) \\ &=5+4(x-2)+3(y-1) \\ &=4x+3y-6 \end{aligned}\] We identify the \(z\)-intercept as \(-6\).

    dm,jb 

  9. Find the equation of the plane tangent to \(z=x^2y+xy^2\) at \((x,y)=(1,2)\). Then find the \(z\)-intercept.

    The tangent plane is \(z=8x+5y-12\).
    The \(z\)-intercept is \(-12\).

    We identify \(a=1\) and \(b=2\): \[\begin{aligned} &f=x^2y+xy^2 \qquad &f(1,2)=6 \\ &f_x=2xy+y^2 \qquad &f_x(1,2)=8 \\ &f_y=x^2+2xy \qquad &f_y(1,2)=5 \end{aligned}\] We now plug this information into the equation for a tangent plane: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f(1,2)+f_x(1,2)(x-1)+f_y(1,2)(y-2) \\ &=6+8(x-1)+5(y-2) \\ &=8x+5y-12 \end{aligned}\] We identify the \(z\)-intercept as \(-12\).

    dm,jb 

  10. Find the equation of the plane tangent to \(z=x^2+y^2\) at the point \((3,1)\). Then, find the \(z\)-intercept of the plane.

    The tangent plane is \(z=6x+2y-10\).
    The \(z\)-intercept is \(-10\).

    First, we find the value of the function, \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2\), at the point \((3,1)\): \[ f(3,1)=3^2+1^2=10 \] Next, we calculate \(f_x\) and \(f_y\) at the point \((3,1)\): \[\begin{aligned} f_x&=2x \qquad f_x(3,1)=6 \\ f_y&=2y \qquad f_y(3,1)=2 \end{aligned}\] Finally, we plug these values into the equation for the tangent plane: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f(3,1)+f_x(3,1)(x-3)+f_y(3,1)(y-1) \\ &=10+6(x-3)+2(y-1)\\ &=6x+2y-10 \end{aligned}\] So the \(z\)-intercept is \(-10\).

    dm,jb 

  11. Find the equation of the tangent plane at the point \((2,4)\) for the function \(z=x^2y-x(y+3)+4\). Then, find the \(z\)-intercept of the plane.

    The tangent line is \(z=9x+2y-20\)
    The \(z\)-interept is \(-20\).

    We evaluate the function, \(f(x,y)=x^2y-x(y+3)+4\), at the point \((2,4)\): \[ f(2,4)=2^24-2(4+3)+4=6 \] Next, we calculate \(f_x\) and \(f_y\) at \((2,4)\): \[\begin{aligned} &f_x=2xy-y-3 \qquad &f_x(2,4)=9 \\ &f_y=x^2-x \qquad &f_y(2,4)=2 \end{aligned}\] Finally, we plug these values into the tangent plane: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f(2,4)+f_x(2,4)(x-2)+f_y(2,4)(y-4) \\ &=6+9(x-2)+2(y-4) \\ &=9x+2y-20 \end{aligned}\] So the \(z\)-interept is \(-20\).

    dm,jb 

  12. Compute the tangent plane to \(z=\sin x\cos y\) at \((x,y)=\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right)\). Then find the \(z\)-intercept.

    What are the values of \(\sin\) and \(\cos\) at \(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\) and \(\dfrac{\pi}{6}\)?

    The tangent plane is: \(z=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}x-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}y +\dfrac{3}{4}-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{24}\)

    The \(z\)-intercept is \(\dfrac{3}{4}-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{24}\).

    The function, its partial derivatives, and their values at \((x,y)=\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right)\) are: \[\begin{aligned} f&=\sin x\cos y \ & f\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) &=\sin\dfrac{\pi}{3}\cos\dfrac{\pi}{6} =\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} =\dfrac{3}{4} \\ f_x&=\cos x\cos y \ & f_x\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) &=\cos\dfrac{\pi}{3}\cos\dfrac{\pi}{6} =\dfrac{1}{2}\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} =\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \\ f_y&=-\sin x\sin y \ & f_y\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) &=-\sin\dfrac{\pi}{3}\sin\dfrac{\pi}{6} =-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\dfrac{1}{2} =-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \end{aligned}\] So the equation of the tangent plane is: \[\begin{aligned} z&=f\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) +f_x\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right)\left(x-\,\dfrac{\pi}{3}\right) +f_y\left(\dfrac{\pi}{3},\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right)\left(y-\,\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) \\ &=\dfrac{3}{4} +\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\left(x-\,\dfrac{\pi}{3}\right) -\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\left(y-\,\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) \\ &=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}x-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}y +\dfrac{3}{4} -\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{12} +\dfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{24} \\ &=\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}x-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}y +\dfrac{3}{4}-\,\dfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{24} \end{aligned}\] We identify the \(z\)-intercept as \(\dfrac{3}{4}-\dfrac{\sqrt{3}\,\pi}{24}\).

    dm,jb

  13. Find \(f_x\), \(f_y\) \(f_z\) for the function \(f=x\sin(yz)\).

    \(f_x=\sin(yz)\)
    \(f_y=xz\cos(yz)\)
    \(f_z=xy\cos(yz)\)

    We differentiate \(f=x\sin(yz)\) with respect to \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\). We get: \[\begin{aligned} f_x&=\sin(yz) \\ f_y&=xz\cos(yz) \\ f_z&=xy\cos(yz) \\ \end{aligned}\]

    dm,jb 

  14. Find the equation of the tangent hyperplane to the function \(w=f(x,y,z)=x^3y^2z\) at the point \((1,2,3)\).

    The tangent hyperplane is: \(w=36x+12y+4z-60\)

    We first find the value of \(f=x\sin(yz)\) at the point \((1,2,3)\): \[ f(1,2,3)=(1^3)(2^2)(3)=12 \] Next, we calculate \(f_x\), \(f_y\) and \(f_z\) at the point \((1,2,3)\): \[\begin{aligned} f_x&=3x^2y^2z \qquad &f_x(1,2,3)&=36 \\ f_y&=2x^3yz \qquad &f_y(1,2,3)&=12 \\ f_z&=x^3y^2 \qquad &f_z(1,2,3)&=4 \end{aligned}\] Then, we plug these into the tangent hyperplane: \[\begin{aligned} w&=f_{\tan}(x,y,z) \\ &=f(1,2,3)+f_x(1,2,3)(x-1)+f_y(1,2,3)(y-2)+f_z(1,2,3)(z-3) \\ &=12+36(x-1)+12(y-2)+4(z-3) \\ &=36x+12y+4z-60 \\ \end{aligned}\]

    dm,jb 

    15. Review Exercises

  15. Find the slopes of the \(x\)-trace and the \(y\)-trace of the function \(f(x,y)=x^3y^4\) at the point \((3,1)\).

    The slope of the \(x\)-trace is \(27\).
    The slope of the \(y\)-trace is \(108\).

    We find the partial derivatives with respect to \(x\) and \(y\) and evaluate at \((3,1)\): \[\begin{aligned} f_x&=3x^2y^4 \qquad &f_x(3,1)&=3(3^2)(1^4)=27 \\ f_y&=4x^3y^3 \qquad &f_y(3,1)&=4(3^3)(1^3)=108 \end{aligned}\] So the slope of the \(x\)-trace is \(27\) and slope of the \(y\)-trace is \(108\).

    dm,jb 

  16. Find the equation of the tangent hyperplane to the function \(w=f(x,y,z)=x^2yz^3\) at the point \((1,3,3)\).

    The tangent hyperplane is: \(w=162x+27y+81z-405\)

    First we find the value of \(f(x,y,z)=x^2yz^3\) at the point \((1,3,3)\): \[ f(1,3,3)=(1^2)(3)(3^3)=81 \] Then we calculate \(f_x(1,3,3)\), \(f_y(1,3,3)\), and \(f_z(1,3,3)\): \[\begin{aligned} f_x&=2xyz^3 \qquad &f_x(1,3,3)&=162 \\ f_y&=x^2z^3 \qquad &f_y(1,3,3)&=27 \\ f_z&=3x^2yz^2 \qquad &f_z(1,3,3)&=81 \end{aligned}\] Finally, we substitute these into the equation of the tangent hyperplane: \[\begin{aligned} w&=f_{\tan}(x,y,z) \\ &=f(1,3,3)+f_x(1,3,3)(x-a)+f_y(1,3,3)(y-b)+f_z(1,3,3)(z-c) \\ &=81+162(x-1)+27(y-3)+81(z-3) \\ &=81+162x-162+27y-81+81z-243 \\ &=162x+27y+81z-405 \\ \end{aligned}\]
